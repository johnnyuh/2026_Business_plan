# Lead Generation & Conversion Funnels
## Canonical Funnels – Full Descriptions

This document defines the standardized lead conversion funnels used across the business. These funnels are intentionally limited in number, reusable across lead sources, and governed by SOPs rather than ad-hoc execution.

---

## Funnel 1: Local Inbound Trust Funnel (Primary)

### Purpose
Convert warm, locally aware prospects into active consultations through trust-building, market insight, and low-friction entry points.

### Primary Lead Sources
- Neighborhood farming (print, signage, posters, QR codes)
- Website content and local landing pages
- Social media (organic)
- YouTube (market commentary, local insights)
- Community presence (buildings, HOAs, events)

### Entry Points
- QR code to landing page
- Website contact form
- Market update opt-in
- Direct message or email inquiry

### Funnel Stages
1. **Awareness**
   - Prospect encounters local market insight or neighborhood-specific messaging.
   - Emphasis on credibility, clarity, and local relevance.

2. **Interest**
   - Prospect opts in for market updates, neighborhood data, or valuation insight.
   - Contact record created in CRM.

3. **Nurture**
   - Automated but human-centered follow-up cadence.
   - Market education, pricing context, and decision framing.
   - No premature sales pressure.

4. **Consultation**
   - Invitation to a structured strategy session (buyer or seller).
   - Discovery, goal alignment, and expectation setting.

5. **Conversion**
   - Client engagement agreement signed.
   - Transition to Client Experience Systems.

### Success Criteria
- High consultation-to-client conversion rate
- Strong trust signals prior to first meeting
- Minimal price resistance due to education-first approach

## Funnel 2: Referral & Sphere Conversion Funnel

### Purpose
Systematize referrals and sphere contacts into predictable, professional conversions without damaging trust or relationships.

### Primary Lead Sources
- Past clients
- Personal sphere
- Professional partners
- Inbound referrals

### Entry Points
- Direct introduction (email, text, verbal)
- Referral form submission
- CRM referral tagging

### Funnel Stages
1. **Introduction**
   - Clear acknowledgment of referral source.
   - Immediate trust inheritance.

2. **Qualification**
   - Light discovery to understand timing, intent, and role of referrer.
   - No pressure or assumption.

3. **Engagement**
   - Tailored outreach aligned to referral context.
   - Reinforcement of professionalism and reliability.

4. **Consultation**
   - Strategy session framed as advisory, not sales.
   - Clear process explanation.

5. **Conversion**
   - Client agreement executed.
   - Referrer loop closed with confirmation and gratitude.

### Success Criteria
- High conversion rate due to pre-existing trust
- Strong referrer satisfaction and repeat referrals
- Consistent communication hygiene

## Funnel 3: Direct Outreach & Reactivation Funnel

### Purpose
Convert cold, dormant, or inactive contacts into live opportunities through relevance and timing—not volume.

### Primary Lead Sources
- CRM dormant contacts
- Expired, withdrawn, or long-term nurture leads
- Geographic or demographic outreach lists

### Entry Points
- Personalized email
- SMS outreach
- Direct mail response
- Phone call (selective, not mass)

### Funnel Stages
1. **Reintroduction**
   - Contextual, respectful re-engagement.
   - Acknowledges time lapse and changing conditions.

2. **Relevance Trigger**
   - Market shift, pricing trend, or situational catalyst.
   - Focus on usefulness, not urgency theater.

3. **Conversation**
   - Two-way engagement re-established.
   - Discovery of updated goals or constraints.

4. **Consultation**
   - Invitation framed as a check-in or planning session.
   - No assumption of readiness.

5. **Conversion**
   - Client agreement if timing aligns.
   - Otherwise, return to nurture track.

### Success Criteria
- Low unsubscribe / opt-out rates
- Recovered pipeline value
- Improved CRM hygiene and clarity

## Funnel 4: Listing-Centric Opportunity Funnel (Situational)

### Purpose
Capture and convert opportunistic demand generated by listings, open houses, and listing-related exposure.

### Primary Lead Sources
- Listing inquiries
- Open house attendees
- Sign calls
- Listing QR codes

### Entry Points
- Open house sign-in
- Property-specific landing pages
- Text-to-info workflows

### Funnel Stages
1. **Property Engagement**
   - Prospect expresses interest in a specific property.
   - Immediate value delivery (details, disclosures, comps).

2. **Context Expansion**
   - Conversation broadens beyond the single property.
   - Education on availability, pricing dynamics, and strategy.

3. **Qualification**
   - Assess seriousness, financing status, and timeline.
   - Respect buyer autonomy.

4. **Consultation**
   - Buyer strategy session or showing plan.
   - Clear explanation of process and representation value.

5. **Conversion**
   - Buyer agency agreement executed.
   - Transition to Buyer Client Experience.

### Success Criteria
- Listing exposure leveraged into multiple opportunities
- Clean separation between listing service and buyer conversion ethics
- Strong post-open-house follow-up discipline

---

## Funnel Governance Notes

- Funnels are **source-agnostic**; lead sources feed funnels, not vice versa.
- All funnels terminate at **Consultation → Conversion**.
- Tactical execution lives in SOPs, not in funnel definitions.
- Funnel performance is reviewed quarterly and adjusted via Growth Gates.

---

## Funnel-to-System Dependencies

- **CRM:** Central system of record
- **SOP Library:** Governs execution
- **Client Experience Systems:** Activated post-conversion
- **Risk Management:** Prevents overextension or quality degradation
