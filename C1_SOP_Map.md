## Purpose
The SOP Map provides a **single visual and conceptual index** of all Standard Operating Procedures governing this business. It clarifies ownership, sequencing, and dependencies across daily execution, client service, marketing, and governance.

This map is a **navigation and control artifact**, not a procedure itself.

---

## SOP Design Principles

- SOPs exist to reduce cognitive load, not eliminate judgment
- Repeatability is prioritized over optimization
- SOPs are reviewed, not continuously edited
- Governance beats improvisation
- Fewer SOPs, executed consistently, outperform complex systems

---

## SOP Categories & Flow

### 1. Strategic & Governance SOPs
These govern **when decisions are allowed** to occur.

- Growth Gate Review (A3)
- Quarterly Growth Review (A6)
- Risk Review & Pause Protocol (A4)
- Monthly Financial Review (C7)

**Trigger:** Calendar-based  
**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Go / Pause / Rollback decisions

---

### 2. Marketing & Funnel SOPs
These govern **how attention is generated and qualified**.

- Content Planning & Batching SOP
- Marketing Calendar Execution (B4)
- Social Content Execution (B5)
- YouTube Anchor Production (B6)

**Trigger:** Weekly cadence  
**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Qualified consultations

---

### 3. Prospecting & Lead Handling SOPs
These govern **how relationships are initiated and progressed**.

- Outbound Prospecting SOP
- Street-Level / Farm Engagement SOP
- Lead Qualification SOP
- Consultation Scheduling SOP

**Trigger:** Daily cadence  
**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Qualified buyer or seller consultations

---

### 4. Client Experience SOPs
These govern **how clients are served and protected**.

- Buyer Client Experience SOP
- Seller Client Experience SOP
- Communication Cadence SOP
- Expectation-Setting SOP

**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Predictable, high-trust experience

---

### 5. Transaction & Execution SOPs

- Contract-to-Close SOP
- Negotiation & Offer Strategy SOP
- Vendor Coordination SOP
- File Management SOP

**Trigger:** Active transaction  
**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Clean closings, reduced error risk

---

### 6. Daily & Weekly Operating SOPs
These govern **execution discipline and recovery**.

- Daily Tactical Schedule (C3)
- Weekly Tactical Checklist (C4)
- Daily Scorecard (C5)
- Weekly Review Template (C6)

**Trigger:** Time-based  
**Owner:** Operator  
**Output:** Consistent execution, early issue detection

---

## SOP Dependency Logic

- Strategic SOPs override all others
- Capacity and recovery SOPs supersede marketing or prospecting
- Client Experience SOPs override volume or speed considerations
- No SOP may be bypassed for urgency without review

---

## SOP Review Cadence

- **Monthly:** Light effectiveness review
- **Quarterly:** Structural alignment review
- **Annual:** Consolidation and pruning only

SOP changes are governed, not reactive.

---

## Executive Rule (Non-Negotiable)

If an SOP adds complexity without reducing risk or load,  
the SOP is removed or simplified.
